### About
bebasid is a project established by the Indonesian Internet Neutral Community (Komunitas Internet Netral Indonesia / KINI) as a learning platform focused on exploring networking in depth. The bebasid project is created and maintained by the community, with each member contributing to its development and continuity.

### Background
As a community, we are interested in developing bebasid to address website blocking in Indonesia. This is due to significant gaps in the blocking system implemented by the Ministry of Communication and Informatics (Kominfo). Despite this, our community also supports Kominfo’s blocking efforts, as they provide benefits for the overall health of Indonesia’s internet ecosystem, particularly in limiting access to content related to gambling and illegal substances.

Another reason for our involvement is that internet blocking in Indonesia poses a serious threat to freedom of expression and democracy. It creates a situation where the state appears to regulate what is permissible or not in internet usage. While other countries are advancing optical networks, 5G technology, and even space-related developments, Indonesia continues to focus on internet blocking with multi-billion budgets driven by moral concerns. Ironically, these systems can still be bypassed using relatively simple applications.

In addition, we have found cases where private entities injected advertisements and services directly into network traffic, even applying them through website CSS. There have also been incidents where cryptocurrency mining scripts were inserted. This is highly disruptive. As a community, we aim to address this through our application, as such actions fall into illegal territory. Users have the right to services they pay for, especially when these modifications were never disclosed or agreed upon and were instead forcibly injected without user knowledge.

### Purpose
The bebasid project is intended to support freedom and neutrality in internet access in Indonesia, as well as to serve as a platform for testing and research into weaknesses within the country’s network systems. Another purpose is to provide a learning environment for understanding networking in Indonesia. Members can use organizational access as a more effective learning medium.

### Benefits
This project is expected to help the government improve, update, and reassess existing systems so that future blocking methods can become more effective. In addition, it is expected that users can use the internet more productively and broaden their knowledge through this project.
